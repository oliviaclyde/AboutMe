Personal Interests

Rim2Rim

In May 2017 I hiked Rim2Rim. This is a grueling, 23.9 mile trek...and we did it in one day. Starting point was the North Rim on the North Kaibab Trail, 5 a.m. We hiked down 14.3 miles and 6,000 feet to Phathom Ranch for lunch. If you are lucky enough to be draw in the lottery they run every year, you could have a chance to stay overnight there. After lunch we crossed the Colorado River. Wow! It was incredible. Then the real work started. Taking Bright Angel Trail we climbed 4,500 feet over 9.6 miles to get back out, arriving at the South Rim at 5 p.m.

Three Things I Learned:

  • Embrace the hard
  • Carry salt tablets
  • Down is optional, Up is required

13.1

I have trained for and completed two half marathons (2014 and 2017). Both these halfs were in June, which meant I started training in January. This meant three-mile runs, three-times a week before work in the dark in freezing temperatures. I'm not afraid of hard things. Just before my long training run for the first half, I suffered an IT band injury. I was able to still race but spent the next year barely able to run a half mile while fighting the flare-up. I learned how to be smart and avoid injury during the training for my second half.

Key Takeaways:

  • Layers are key
  • Do the long run
  • Be cognizant of injury potential
